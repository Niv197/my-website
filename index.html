From bar management to QA: the surprising connection

During my role as a bar manager, I developed essential skills that are highly relevant to the field of software testing. Every evening before opening, I adhered to organized work procedures that included inventory checks, drink temperature checks, and ensuring equipment functionality. This meticulous organization and attention to detail allowed me to enhance the customer experience and provide high-level service. In this role, I was required to identify and solve real-time issues, such as equipment malfunctions or inventory problems, to prevent any impact on the customer experience.

Managing the team of bartenders and overseeing the shift were integral parts of my job. I ensured that I guided and supervised the staff, making sure they performed their tasks accurately and efficiently, while maintaining open and effective communication among all team members. These skills are directly applicable to the QA field, where teamwork and good communication are essential to ensure that every issue is documented and resolved promptly. The ability to lead and manage a team while solving real-time problems is an important skill in managing software testing processes, where prioritizing and managing issues and solutions in an organized and professional manner is necessary.

The ability to maintain a high standard of quality and focus on the details, combined with strong communication skills, teamwork, and crisis management, gives me an advantage in the world of software testing and enables me to contribute to continuous improvement in product and process quality.
